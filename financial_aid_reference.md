## PART 1 — FEDERAL AID MECHANICS

### FAFSA / SAI
- Simplified from 108 → 46 questions; SAI replaces EFC (floor: −$1,500)
- Mandatory IRS Direct Data Exchange (no more voluntary retrieval)
- **Asset protection allowance: $0** — eliminated entirely
- **Multiple students in college: no longer reduces SAI** (major loss for close-in-age siblings)
- Divorced/separated: reporting parent = who provided more financial support in prior 12 months (not custody)

### Pell Grant (2025–2026)
- Maximum: **$7,395** / Minimum: **$740**
- Lifetime limit: **12 semesters (600% LEU)**
- 150% rule: up to $11,092.50 in one year (accelerates LEU consumption)
- **July 1, 2026**: Pell ineligible if non-federal grant/scholarship aid ≥ COA (affects full-ride recipients)

### Federal Loan Interest Rates (2025–2026)

| Direct Unsubsidized (Grad) | 7.94% | 9.50% |
| Direct PLUS | 8.94% | 10.50% |
| PLUS origination fee | 4.228% | — |

### Loan Limits — Pre/Post OBBBA
| Borrower | Annual | Aggregate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dependent UG | Unchanged | $31,000 | |
| Independent UG | Unchanged | $57,500 | |
| Graduate | $20,500 | **$100,000** (eff. 7/1/26) | Grad PLUS eliminated |
| Professional (11 fields only) | $50,000 | **$200,000** | See field list below |
| Parent PLUS | **$20,000/child** | **$65,000/child** | No IDR for new loans after 7/1/26 |

**Professional degree = exactly 11 fields**: medicine, dentistry, optometry, osteopathic medicine, pharmacy, podiatry, chiropractic, clinical psychology, law, theology, veterinary medicine. MBA, MPH, MSW, engineering — NOT included; capped at $20,500/year.

### Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)
- GPA: minimum 2.0 cumulative
- Pace: complete ≥67% of attempted credits
- Timeframe: complete within 150% of normal program length

### Professional Judgment
- Aid administrator's decision is final — school president and US DOE cannot override
- Successful triggers: job loss (strongest), medical costs, competing peer offers
- Two categories: Special Circumstances (adjust SAI inputs) and Unusual Circumstances (dependency override)
- Dependency overrides for: abuse, abandonment, trafficking, incarceration, refugee/asylee status

## PART 2 — REPAYMENT AND FORGIVENESS

### Repayment Plan Landscape
| Plan | Status | Payment | Forgiveness |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard (10-yr) | Available for existing borrowers; eliminated for new loans after 7/1/26 | Fixed | None |
| IBR (old, pre-7/1/14) | **Permanent** | 15% discretionary | 25 years |
| IBR (new, post-7/1/14) | **Permanent** | 10% discretionary | 20 years |
| PAYE | Sunsets **7/1/2028** | 10% discretionary | 20 years |
| SAVE | **VACATED** Dec 2025 | — | — |
| **RAP** (new) | Launches **7/1/2026** | 1–10% AGI (see formula) | **30 years** |

### RAP Formula (Exact)
```
Monthly payment = [(AGI × Rate) ÷ 12] − ($50 × number of dependents)
Rate = min(10%, floor(AGI / $10,000) + 1%)
```
| AGI | Rate | Monthly (no dependents) |
|---|---|---|
| $20k–$29,999 | 3% | $50–$75 |
| $40k–$49,999 | 5% | $167–$208 |
| $60k–$69,999 | 7% | $350–$408 |
| $80k–$89,999 | 9% | $600–$675 |
| $90k+ | 10% | $750+ |

- Government subsidizes any shortfall below $50 principal reduction per payment
- Unpaid interest does NOT capitalize
- Minimum payment: $10/month

**RAP vs. IBR**: For most borrowers above $40k AGI, IBR is superior (shorter forgiveness, lower payments if discretionary income < AGI). RAP is primarily better at very low incomes needing the $10 minimum floor.

### PSLF
- 120 qualifying payments at qualifying employer → full forgiveness, permanently tax-free
- Current stats: $87.6B discharged · 93% of applications denied (vs. 99.7% in 2017–2020) · 26% denials = incomplete paperwork
- **PSLF buyback paused** January 2025; 72,730 pending cases; **10-month wait times**
- **July 1, 2026**: Employer disqualification for "substantial illegal purpose" (narrow: immigration law violations, terrorism support, minor castration, minor trafficking, repeated employment law violations)
- Download and preserve your NSLDS payment history independently — do not rely on servicer displays

### Teacher Loan Forgiveness
- $17,500: math, science, special education (high-need schools, 5 consecutive years)
- $5,000: all other qualifying teachers
- Cannot simultaneously pursue PSLF — choose one

### State Loan Forgiveness
- 45 states + DC offer programs; amounts up to $300,000 (NY, MI healthcare)
- Healthcare, legal/public interest, teaching most common targets
- HRSA federal programs available nationally for healthcare

### Loan Forgiveness Tax Treatment (CRITICAL — Changed Jan 1, 2026)
- **IDR forgiveness**: FULLY TAXABLE as Cancellation of Debt income starting 2026 (ARPA expired 12/31/25)
- **PSLF**: Permanently tax-free
- **Teacher Loan Forgiveness**: Permanently tax-free
- **5 states tax even PSLF**: Arkansas, Indiana, Mississippi, North Carolina, Wisconsin
- Example: $100k IDR forgiveness in 2026 → ~$24k federal + ~$5k state = $29k tax bill with no cash income

### Default and Recovery
- Default = 270+ days; federal collections **resumed May 5, 2025** (wage garnishment up to 15%, tax refund offset)
- Rehabilitation (9 months): removes default from credit report entirely; one-time limit (two-time for loans after 7/1/27)
- Consolidation (4–8 weeks): faster but default history stays 7 years on credit report
- Bankruptcy: file adversary complaint + sworn DOJ Attestation Form (present/persistent inability to pay + good faith)

### Section 127 (Employer Student Loan Assistance)
- $5,250/year tax-free, **made permanent by OBBBA**
- **Inflation-indexed** starting 2026
- Covers tuition AND student loan repayment payments

## PART 3 — INSTITUTIONAL AID

### Discount Rates (2024–2025)
| Institution Type | Avg Discount Rate |
|---|---|
| Elite private/liberal arts | 75–85% |
| Private research universities | 55–65% |
| Public flagship | ~29% |
| Public non-flagship | ~17% |
| Overall private average | 56.3% (record) |

Net tuition revenue rose only 1.4% despite record discounting. Schools use prescriptive analytics to model exact aid offers that maximize yield at minimum cost.

### CSS Profile vs. FAFSA
| Element | FAFSA | CSS Profile |
|---|---|---|
| Primary home equity | Excluded | Included (caps vary: 1.2x–2.0x income) |
| Non-custodial parent | Excluded | Required (at full IM schools) |
| Business assets (any size) | Excludes <100 employees | Included |
| Grandparent 529 | 0% impact | School-dependent |

IM generates 20–40% higher expected contribution than FAFSA for middle-income families with assets.

### Packaging Strategies
- **Preferential packaging**: Top 25% admits get grant-heavy, no-loan packages; remaining 75% get loan-heavy
- **Front-loading**: 75% of first-years receive grants vs. 63% of upper-class — 12-point systematic drop
- **Gapping**: Most common for $60k–$150k income families; elite privates rarely gap
- **Appeals succeed ~33%** when well-documented; job loss is highest-success trigger
- Schools known to match competing offers: Cornell, Penn; most publics refuse
- Frame appeals as "compare with similar schools" not "match this offer"

### 568 Group
- Formally disbanded 2019 (antitrust challenge) but former ~28 members continue Consensus Methodology informally
- Requires non-custodial parent info, detailed home equity/business documentation beyond CSS Profile

### Needs-Blind Admissions (~12 true schools)
All 8 Ivies, MIT, Amherst, Grinnell, Washington and Lee, Caltech. All others are need-aware to varying degrees.

### Real-Time Fraud Detection (April 26, 2026)
Every FAFSA applicant screened at submission via financial-services identity verification. High-risk: Reject Code 74 / Comment Code 355 — must show photo ID via live video before aid disburses. $1B+ fraud prevented; ~150,000 suspicious identities flagged.

### Return to Title IV (R2T4)
When student withdraws:
- Earned aid % = days attended ÷ days in payment period
- Unearned portion returns to federal programs within 30 days
- Completely independent of institutional refund policy — two separate calculations

## PART 4 — TAX BENEFITS AND SAVINGS VEHICLES

### Education Tax Credits (2025–2026)
| | AOTC | LLC |
|---|---|---|
| Max credit | $2,500/student | $3,000/return (OBBBA ↑ from $2,000) |
| Refundable | 40% (up to $1,000) | No |
| Year limit | First 5 years (OBBBA ↑ from 4) | Unlimited |
| Income phaseout (single) | $90k–$110k | $80k–$90k |
| Income phaseout (MFJ) | $180k–$220k | $160k–$180k |
| Covers | Tuition, fees, books, supplies | Tuition, fees, credentialing |

**AOTC/scholarship election**: IRS Pub 970 allows electing to include otherwise tax-free scholarship in taxable income to increase QHEEs for AOTC. Almost always worth analyzing — most preparers miss it.

**Example**: Student has $8k tuition, $6k scholarship. Without election: $2k QHEEs → $2,000 credit. With election (include $5k in student income): $8k QHEEs → $2,500 credit. $500 credit gain minus student's tax on $5k at 10% = net positive.

**Cannot claim both AOTC + LLC** for same student in same year. AOTC generally better for undergrads; LLC for graduate/professional students.

### Student Loan Interest Deduction
- $2,500 above-the-line (no itemizing required)
- Phaseout: $85k–$100k (single), $170k–$200k (MFJ) in 2025

### 529 Plans
**Contribution**: No federal annual limit. Superfunding: $95,000 individual / $190,000 married using 5-year gift tax averaging (file Form 709 year 1 only).

**⚠️ Superfunding death trap**: If donor dies during 5-year period, remaining years must be included in gross estate. Do not use for elderly or seriously ill donors.

**FAFSA impact by owner**:
| Owner | Assessment Rate |
|---|---|
| Parent | 5.64% |
| Grandparent | **0%** (post-2024) |
| Student | 20% |

**Qualified expenses (2025–2026)**:
- Tuition, fees, books, supplies, room & board (half-time+)
- K-12: up to **$20,000/year** (OBBBA doubled from $10k)
- Student loans: $10,000 lifetime
- Registered apprenticeships
- Qualified postsecondary credentialing

**⚠️ State recapture trap**: States that don't recognize K-12 as qualified (CT, IL, HI, MI, MN, MT, NM, NY, OR, VT) will claw back deductions if OBBBA's K-12 expansion is used.

**529 → Roth IRA (SECURE 2.0)**:
- Lifetime limit: $35,000
- Account must be ≥15 years old
- Contributions ≥5 years old only
- Counts toward annual Roth contribution limit ($7,500 in 2026)
- **UNRESOLVED**: Does changing beneficiary reset the 15-year clock? IRS has issued no guidance. Conservative position: yes, it resets.

### State 529 Tax Parity (can deduct for ANY state's plan)
Arizona, Arkansas, Colorado, Kansas, **Minnesota**, Missouri, Montana, Ohio, Pennsylvania.

Minnesota's unique structure: 50% credit up to $500 OR $1,500 deduction (no income limit for deduction).

### Section 2503(e): The Unlimited Strategy
Direct tuition payments paid directly to an educational institution are **exempt from gift tax entirely** — unlimited, separate from $19k annual exclusion, no Form 709 required. Only tuition qualifies (not room/board, books, computers).

**Optimal grandparent strategy**: Pay tuition directly ($40k/year, unlimited gift-tax-free) + fund grandparent-owned 529 ($95k superfund, 0% FAFSA impact) = $135k annual transfer, entirely gift-tax-free, zero aid impact.

### Coverdell ESA
- $2,000/year aggregate per beneficiary
- Broader expenses than 529 (K-12 uniforms, transportation, extended-day)
- Income limit: $95k–$110k (single), $190k–$220k (MFJ)
- Must use by age 30

### UGMA/UTMA Custodial Accounts
- **20% FAFSA assessment** (worst category — avoid for education savings)
- Kiddie tax applies to unearned income above $2,700 (taxed at parent's rate)
- $60k UTMA reduces aid by $12k/year vs. $3,384 for parent-owned 529

### Trust Assets
- **Always assessed at 20%** regardless of trustee discretion or access restrictions
- Sole exception: Special Needs Trusts restricted by court order (not grantor discretion)
- Trusts are excellent estate planning tools but actively harmful for aid optimization

### ABLE Accounts (for Disabled Beneficiaries)
- **Age-of-onset raised to 46** (effective January 1, 2026; was 26)
- $19,000/year contribution limit (2025); employed beneficiaries add $15,650
- First $100,000 exempt from $2,000 SSI resource limit
- SSI suspends if ABLE exceeds $100k (but Medicaid continues)

### Savings Bonds for Education
- Series EE/I: tax-free interest when used for tuition/fees at eligible institutions
- Bonds must be in your name (not child's); you must be 24+ when issued
- Income phaseout 2025: $101,800–$116,800 (single), $149,250–$179,250 (MFJ)

### Work-Study's Hidden FAFSA Advantage
Federal Work-Study earnings are **excluded from the subsequent year's FAFSA** as student income. Regular student employment earnings count. Routing employment through FWS saves 20% of earnings in aid reduction the following year.

## PART 6 — SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### DACA / Undocumented Students (Active Litigation Landscape, 2025)
- No federal Title IV aid (DACA or undocumented)
- **Texas**: Dream Act ended June 2025 (DOJ consent judgment)
- **Kentucky**: In-state tuition ended August 2025
- **California**: DOJ filed federal suit November 21, 2025 challenging AB 540
- **States still offering aid** (as of late 2025): CA (contested), CO, IL, MD, MA, MN, NV, NJ, NM, NY, OR, VT, VA, WA, DC + others
- Private institutional aid available regardless of documentation at most private schools

### Foster Care
- Chafee ETV: up to $5,000/year through age 26 (5-year maximum)
- Automatic independent status on FAFSA
- 37 states have tuition waivers; Texas removed age cap June 2025 (HB 1211)

### Incarcerated Students
- Second Chance Pell became permanent Prison Education Programs (PEP) July 1, 2023
- 7,000+ students earned degrees/certificates in first 4 years
- OBBBA did not materially change Second Chance Pell

### Students with Disabilities
- **SSI is NOT reportable on FAFSA; SSDI IS** (as untaxed income) — commonly confused
- TPD discharge: $18.7B approved for 633,000 borrowers 2020–2025; 3-year monitoring period eliminated (2021)
- Professional judgment can offset SSDI income with documented disability-related costs
- ABLE accounts: age-of-onset raised to 46 effective Jan 2026

### Graduate and Professional School
- PhD programs: funded by tuition waivers + stipends ($25k–$35k/year) via assistantships — these are employment, not aid
- NSF GRFP: $37,000/year stipend + $16,000 cost-of-education; **2026 budget request cut 55%**; clinical psychology excluded
- NHSC Scholarship: full tuition + stipend; 2025–26 window March 27 – May 8
- IHS Scholarship: federally recognized AI/AN only; full tuition + $1,500+/month
- Law school LRAPs: HYS most generous; NYU covers up to $110k income; Columbia forgives after 3+ years public interest

### Athletic Aid
- NCAA 2025–26: all sports now equivalency-based (headcount sports eliminated); roster limits replace scholarship caps
- Football: 85 old scholarships → 105 roster spots; baseball: 11.7 → 34 roster spots
- NIL income does not reduce athletic scholarships but increases AGI, potentially reducing need-based aid
- NIL income >$400/year requires federal tax filing
